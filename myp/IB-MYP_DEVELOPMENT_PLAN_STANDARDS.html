IB/MYP DEVELOPMENT PLAN STANDARDS

STANDARD 1: The school’s pedagogical leadership team adopts teaching approaches that encourage students to become active, compassionate lifelong learners.

  • Implement teaching and learning strategies:

    • Examples: Photos/videos of PD sessions on inquiry, peer observation feedback forms, presentation slides from staff meetings on new teaching methods.
    • Relevant Evidence: Photos, videos, documents (feedback forms, presentations).

  • Submit documents:

    • Examples: Copies of curriculum documents (subject descriptions, unit plans, service as action), ATL framework, authentic assessment tasks.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (curriculum, ATL, assessment tasks).

  • Set up regular collaboration:

    • Examples: Meeting agendas and minutes of collaborative sessions, shared online folders with teaching resources.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (agendas, minutes), online exchanges.

STANDARD 2: Teachers focus on conceptual understanding to help students develop their ideas.

  • Apply deductive model:

    • Examples: Lesson plans showing deductive progression, student work demonstrating application of general principles to specific examples.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (lesson plans, student work).

  • Use concept maps:

    • Examples: Photos of student-created concept maps (physical or digital), rubrics used for assessing concept map understanding.
    • Relevant Evidence: Photos, documents (rubrics), activities.

  • Frayer-model activities:

    • Examples: Completed Frayer Models by students, photos of anchor charts displaying Frayer Models.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (completed models), photos.

  • Concept-based inquiry:

    • Examples: Student inquiry questions, research proposals, experiment logs, reflection journals on inquiry process.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (proposals, logs, journals), activities.

  • Integrate contextual activities:

    • Examples: Photos/videos of simulations or role-plays, student reflections on real-world connections.
    • Relevant Evidence: Photos, videos, documents (reflections), activities.

STANDARD 3: Teachers encourage students to apply conceptual understanding in new contexts.

  • Use prior knowledge:

    • Examples: Diagnostic test results, brainstorming notes, pre-assessment quizzes, student self-assessment sheets.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (test results, notes, quizzes, self-assessments).

  • Select relevant contexts/questions:

    • Examples: Unit plans showing factual, conceptual, and debatable questions, chosen contexts.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (unit plans).

  • Develop learning activities:

    • Examples: Photos/videos of students engaged in activities connecting subject knowledge to concepts, activity handouts.
    • Relevant Evidence: Photos, videos, documents (handouts), activities.

  • Create interactive environments:

    • Examples: Photos/videos of interactive learning stations, screenshots of digital interactive tools used.
    • Relevant Evidence: Photos, videos, online exchanges (screenshots).

  • Offer differentiated learning:

    • Examples: Differentiated activity sheets, examples of varied assessment options for students.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (activity sheets, assessment examples).

  • Ensure interdisciplinary teaching:

    • Examples: Joint project plans with other subjects, student work showing connections across disciplines.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (project plans, student work).

  • Develop authentic summative assessments:

    • Examples: Rubrics for "BUILD" process assessments, examples of completed authentic projects.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (rubrics, completed projects).

  • Enrich subject content:

    • Examples: Photos/videos of guest speakers or field trips, examples of supplementary resources used.
    • Relevant Evidence: Photos, videos, documents (resources), activities.

STANDARD 4: Students and teachers use feedback to improve learning, teaching, and assessment.

  • Provide feedback:

    • Examples: Marked assignments with teacher comments, screenshots of digital feedback platforms, audio/video recordings of feedback conferences.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (marked assignments), online exchanges (screenshots), videos, recordings.

  • Clarify assessment criteria:

    • Examples: Photos of assessment rubrics displayed, student reflections on assessment criteria understanding.
    • Relevant Evidence: Photos, documents (reflections), activities.

  • Promote self-assessment:

    • Examples: Completed student self-assessment checklists, journal entries reflecting on learning.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (checklists, journal entries).

  • Use self/peer-assessment grids:

    • Examples: Completed self-assessment grids, peer feedback forms.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (grids, forms).

  • Implement tracking for students in difficulty:

    • Examples: Personalized follow-up sheets, adapted assessment examples.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (follow-up sheets, assessment examples).

  • Plan variety of assessments:

    • Examples: Unit plans detailing diverse assessment types, pre-assessment results informing assessment choices.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (unit plans, test results).

STANDARD 5: Students take ownership of their learning by setting challenging goals and pursuing personal inquiry.

  • Vary group work activities:

    • Examples: Photos/videos of students in homogeneous/heterogeneous groups, group project plans.
    • Relevant Evidence: Photos, videos, documents (project plans).

  • Organize presentations/exhibitions:

    • Examples: Photos/videos of student presentations, exhibition displays, event programs.
    • Relevant Evidence: Photos, videos, documents (programs), activities.

  • Use digital teaching tools:

    • Examples: Screenshots of digital tools in use, student projects created with digital software.
    • Relevant Evidence: Online exchanges (screenshots), documents (student projects).

  • Promote ICT use:

    • Examples: Student research logs showing online database usage, examples of digital support activities.
    • Relevant Evidence: Photos, videos, activities, factsheets (data).

  • Implement active learning strategies:

    • Examples: Photos/videos of "jigsaw" or "think-pair-share" activities, student engagement data.
    • Relevant Evidence: Photos, videos, activities, factsheets (data).

  • Base activities on competencies:

    • Examples: Lesson plans highlighting targeted student competencies, student work demonstrating competency development.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (lesson plans, student work).

STANDARD 6: Students actively develop thinking, research, communication, social, and self-management skills.

  • Use varied strategies:

    • Examples: Photos/videos of cooperative work, mind mapping sessions, game-based learning, problem-solving activities.
    • Relevant Evidence: Photos, videos, activities.

  • Integrate service as action:

    • Examples: Service as action project proposals, photos/videos of student involvement in actions, action sheets.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (proposals, action sheets), photos, videos, activities.

  • Students record actions and reflect:

    • Examples: Completed student action sheets with reflections, student reflection journals.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (action sheets, journals).

  • Ensure impactful student projects:

    • Examples: Photos/videos of completed student projects addressing real needs, project impact reports.
    • Relevant Evidence: Photos, videos, documents (reports), activities.

  • Implement student monitoring sheet:

    • Examples: Completed student project engagement monitoring sheets.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (monitoring sheets).

  • Provide structured guidance:

    • Examples: Workshop materials on project management, mentor meeting logs for personal projects.
    • Relevant Evidence: Documents (materials, logs), meetings, references.
